1. Personal Legal Notice

This document constitutes formal notice asserting rights under UK GDPR, Data Protection Act 2018, Protection from Harassment Act 1997, and common law privacy principles.

Valuation of Time

Time spent responding to disputes shall be valued at a significantly uplifted rate reflecting distress and disruption, with a minimum of 10× UK National Minimum Wage.

5. ANPR and Parking Enforcement Defence

This section addresses the use of Automatic Number Plate Recognition (ANPR) systems and private or public parking enforcement practices.

Position on ANPR Surveillance

The Individual objects to the use of ANPR systems where such use is not demonstrably necessary, proportionate, and compliant with UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018.

  • ANPR data constitutes personal data when linked to an identifiable individual
  • Processing must have a lawful basis
  • Data collection must be transparent and clearly communicated

Key Challenges to Enforcement

  • Lack of clear and visible signage at the point of entry
  • Failure to establish a lawful basis for data processing
  • Disproportionate or excessive data retention
  • Failure to demonstrate contractual agreement

Data Rights Assertion

The Individual reserves the right to:

  • Request all ANPR data held (via SAR)
  • Challenge the accuracy of time-stamps and records
  • Request erasure where no lawful basis exists

Response Template (Parking Charge Notice)

To: [Parking Operator]

I dispute the validity of this Parking Charge Notice.

Please provide:
- Evidence of lawful basis for data processing
- Proof of adequate signage and contract formation
- Full ANPR data records relating to this incident

Any processing of my personal data must comply with UK GDPR.

I reserve all rights, including the right to seek damages for misuse of personal data and distress.

Compensation Position

Where enforcement action results in distress, time expenditure, or legal defence, the Individual may seek recovery of costs consistent with the valuation principles outlined in this document.
